About

I build and implement computational tools for social good. At the moment, this means working in AI Safety and AI for Climate. In the former, I seek to make models resistant to malicious manipulation while ensuring their behavior is aligned with human values, drawing on ideas from adversarial robustness, alignment, and interpretability while advocating for technical AI governance. In the latter, I aim to apply the most powerful technical tools of our time to one of the most pressing global issues of our time, with interests in the East African Climate Paradox and Energy Optimization. Read more at my blog.


I graduated from the University of Oxford with master's degrees in Mathematics and Linguistics, where I studied as a Rhodes Scholar. Prior to Oxford, I earned degrees in Mathematics and German from the University of Alabama, with some side quests into physics, psychology, and computer science. I spent an academic year in Tanzania as a Boren Scholar, where I earned a fluency certificate in Swahili. For my CV: professional | fun.
