About

My interests are very broad, but generally united by an aim to do social and environmental good. In the past, this has included dolphin vocalization research, political discourse analysis, freelance translating, and data science for a global security nonprofit. At the moment, this means working on AI Safety and AI for Climate. In the former, I seek to make models resistant to malicious manipulation while ensuring their behavior is aligned with human values, drawing on ideas from adversarial robustness, alignment, and interpretability while advocating for technical AI governance. In the latter, I aim to apply the most powerful technical tools of our time to one of the most pressing global issues of our time, with interests in the East African Climate Paradox, Energy Optimization, and Natural Disaster Mitigation. Read more about my work at my blog.


I earned master's degrees in Mathematics and Linguistics from Oxford, where I studied as a Rhodes Scholar. Prior to Oxford, I earned degrees in Mathematics and German from the University of Alabama, with some side quests into physics, psychology, and computer science. I also spent an academic year in Tanzania as a Boren Scholar, where I earned a fluency certificate in Swahili. For my CV: professional | fun.

yo-yoer (1A, 5A), speedcuber, aspiring mandolinist, ultramarathoner, cyclist, language enthusiast, and writer.
