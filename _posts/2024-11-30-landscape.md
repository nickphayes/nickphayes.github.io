## AI Safety

**Interpretability**
Interpretability researchers seek to understand and explain how models actually work. While AI capabilities researchers continue pushing the field forward in terms of what we are capable of building, we don't have a cogent understanding of how or why frontier models actually work. We have a rough idea of what information models can learn from (the data we provide), how we allow information to propagate throughout the system (the model architecture we design), and what kind of behavior is rewarded/penalized (the loss/reward functions we define), but we don't know *what* or *how* the model learns. 

It's a bit like watching a newborn learn a new skill, like walking or talking. Ostensibly, a newborn exists in an environment where other people are walking/talking. We have a general idea of what information they can receive (sensory input), as well as how information might propagate (i.e. light reaches photoreceptors in the retina, converted electrical signals are sent to the brain via the optic nerve, etc.). Socialization norms encourage newborns to try walking/talking, while discouraging other behaviors (e.g. eating things you aren't supposed to)
